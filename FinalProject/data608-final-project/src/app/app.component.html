Projected temperature warming
in the 21st Century
under two different worlds

This set of visualizations are based on temperature data derived from multiple global circulation models (GCMs), aggregated to the country level for months and years in four future time periods. The ensemble data depict the median range of model outputs run under each of two scenarios, "A2" and "B1". More data is available via the Climate Data API, documented at http://data.worldbank.org/developers/climate-data-api.

Scenario B1: Models temperature predictions from a more integrated, ecologically friendly world based on:

  • Rapid economic growth, but with rapid changes towards a service and information economy.
  • Population rising to 9 billion in 2050 and then declining.
  • Reductions in material intensity and the introduction of clean and resource efficient technologies.
  • An emphasis on global solutions to economic, social and environmental stability.

Scenario A2: Models temperature predictions from a more divided world based on:

  • A world of independently operating, self-reliant nations.
  • Continuously increasing population.
  • Regionally oriented economic development.
  • High emissions.
